The Scale of Impact

A Polish Educational Staple

With over 100,000 users, Wolne Lektury serves as the primary gateway to the Polish literary canon. It’s more than just an app; it’s an essential utility for literacy.

A digital library stuck in the past

Despite its essential role, the app was stuck in a previous era of mobile design. I started this project as a reader like myself, frustrated by an interface that felt more like a technical database than a library.

I wanted to see if others felt the same. Through my research, I found that the "aging" interface wasn't just a visual issue—it was creating a functional barrier within education.

01 / THE PROBLEM

The Friction of Literacy

Classic literature is inherently demanding. When the digital medium adds "chaotic menus" and "confusing filters" to an already difficult task, users simply give up. I found that even with a strong need for accessible classics, the current friction was discouraging users from the books themselves.

Fig 01. Analyzing the navigation debt in the original application.

02 / RESEARCH FINDINGS

Design as a Trust Signal

First impressions are 94% influenced by design. I conducted usability tests with 5 users to audit the current state and faced a staggering reality: 80% of users had a negative first reaction. The problem wasn't the content, but the vessel carrying it.

The 3.8-star rating on the App Store wasn't due to poor content—it was a reflection of the friction users faced before the first chapter even loaded.

I.

Color Overload

Too many competing colors distracted from the reading experience.

II.

Legibility

III.

Feature Fog

Users were confused by basic features, such as the "Patron" system.

03 / USER PERSONA

User Persona: The Time-Poor Student

While the library is open to everyone, Wolne Lektury targets students as their core audience. The primary pain points are felt most by users like Maciek. For him, the app isn't just a library; it's a tool that needs to compete with a busy work-study schedule.

Maciek, 17

Student Unmotivated Busy

"I have 15 minutes to find a reference—if the app is a maze, I’m out."

Primary Need

A streamlined reading dashboard that prioritizes Progress Tracking and Instant Library Access to maximize limited study windows.

04 / PROTOTYPING

The Iterative Loop

I initially sketched solutions that stayed close to the original layout to avoid "re-learning" costs for existing users. Testing revealed that users felt motivated by the progress bars and specifically appreciated reading time estimates, as it helped them schedule their reading around their classes. However, the overall navigation still felt cluttered.

UX INSIGHT

Mental Model Conflict

While testing the original hero section, I noticed a consistent pattern of hesitation. Users didn't know if the highlighted book was "theirs" or an "advertisement."

The Confusion

"I see a book cover here, but I don't know if clicking it will take me to my last read or to a shop. I'm afraid to tap it."

The Solution

Replaced the "Featured" slot with a Personalized Dashboard that uses progress bars to signal ownership and status.

The Logic Shift

Old Flow: 5 Steps (Home > Library > Category > Author > Book)

New Flow: 1 Step (Home > Resume Reading)

I wanted to put Statistics section at the top but it turned out to be too disruptive to the flow.

Iteration 01: Feedback was harsh but necessary—"Button names are unclear," and "The scroll is endless."

Accessibility Gap

Low color contrast and rigid text blocks of my initial redesign made the app difficult for users with visual impairments or dyslexia.

Applying Miller's Law

To reduce Cognitive Load, I implemented Progressive Disclosure. By truncating filters and only showing secondary information when requested, I allowed Maciek to focus on one decision at a time.

The original navigation relied on a deep, nested hierarchy that forced students to navigate through multiple sub-menus just to resume a book. I restructured the Information Architecture to prioritize Recency and Accessibility. By flattening the navigation, I ensured that 'Active Reads' and 'Search' were always one tap away.

05 / IMPACT

Impact & Conclusion

Structure creates trust. I stripped away the visual clutter of the original design to prioritize functional clarity. This shift didn't just organize the app; it empowered users to actually utilize the features they once ignored due to poor labeling and layout chaos.

Takeaway: Adhering to design principles is essential, but it is no substitute for deep research. Through this process, I learned that even the most 'correct' UI can fail if it doesn't align with the user's core goals. Sometimes the solution isn't a better button—it’s a new functionality altogether. UX research doesn't just polish an app; it defines the roadmap for what should be built next.
