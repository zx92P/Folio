01 / DISCOVERY

The Vanishing Interface

Right from the first boot, I knew something was wrong. An October 2021 update remarkably removed the core menu button. My intuition was confirmed by 600+ reviews averaging 2 stars. The official mobile app, around since 2015, had effectively locked its users out of their own experience.

02 / RESEARCH

02.5 / COMPETITIVE AUDIT

Mapping the Battlefield

To understand where the current experience was failing, I audited four leading sports competitors. While these platforms vary in scale, they share a common goal: delivering high-stakes data in seconds. By analyzing thousands of App Store reviews and mapping their features, I identified the industry "habits" that users have come to expect—and exactly where our app was breaking those promises.

Strengths

  • Official brand authority
  • Exclusive behind-the-scenes content

Weaknesses

  • Outdated UI & Low Functionality
  • Difficult & Unintuitive Navigation

Opportunities

  • Streamlined live-score tracking
  • Personalized "My Team" dashboards

Threats

  • User Churn due to Negative Reviews
  • Competitors Delivering Superior Effort

The SWOT analysis served as a strategic compass, ensuring that my redesign wasn't just fixing bugs, but building a moat against more nimble competitors.

04 / ITERATION

Ideation & A/B Testing

I used 'Crazy 8’s' to ideate rapidly. My first designs actually failed—they created new issues. I dove back into wireframing and settled on a design that leveraged existing mobile habits. Users settled on a layout because it felt familiar and offered easy access to data.

The final iteration focused on familiarity. Users settled on Variant B because it offered the fastest path to live match data without sacrificing brand aesthetic.

Logic

Thumb-Zone: Core menu moved to bottom nav for one-handed use on job sites.

Logic

Familiarity: Standardized 'Home' and 'Search' icons to reduce learning curves.

Logic

Priority: Sticky "Live Now" header satisfies the need for immediate data.

User Reservation: While highly efficient, some users felt the "Exclusive Content" was deprioritized. However, 100% of participants agreed that score accessibility was the primary goal.

05 / IMPACT

Impact & Conclusion

By bringing order and symmetry, time on task decreased significantly and confusion dropped. Sometimes the best solutions come from studying others' designs to ensure familiarity and decrease user fatigue.

Takeaway: Don’t be scared to start over; there is no point in forcing a concept that is wrong at its core. Instead, let your creativity flow and embrace imperfections, as the best solutions often arrive when you least expect them. Draw inspiration from established designs to build familiarity and reduce user fatigue, but never stop validating—test everything, always, because there is no such thing as too much testing.
