The Drive Behind The Project

A Failure of Official Standards

Wanting to follow the Polish national team, I turned to the official platform. What I found was a design so broken it prompted 600+ reviews averaging 2 stars. A 2021 update had deleted core navigation features, effectively locking fans out.

01 / DISCOVERY

First Impressions vs. Reality

My intuition was immediate: the app felt stuck in 2015. To validate this, I audited recent updates and store feedback. The data was clear—the "digital home" of Polish football was causing massive user friction. This wasn't just a visual issue; it was a functional lockout.

"Who even let this out to use? Where’s the menu, where is the login which is required by the app? Nothing works here."

02.5 / COMPETITIVE AUDIT

Learning from the Competition

To understand where the current experience was failing, I audited four leading sports competitors. While these platforms vary in scale, they share a common goal: delivering live match updates in seconds. By analyzing thousands of App Store reviews and mapping their features, I identified the industry "habits" that users have come to expect—and exactly where our app was breaking those promises.

Task: Check Live Score of "Team X"

Strengths

  • Exclusive behind-the-scenes content

Weaknesses

  • Difficult & Unintuitive Navigation

Threats

  • Competitors Delivering Superior Effort

The SWOT analysis served as a strategic compass, ensuring that my redesign wasn't just fixing bugs, but building a moat against more nimble competitors.

03 / USER PERSONA

The Exhausted Supporter

Understanding the "Environmental Context" was key. A fan like Tomek isn't browsing in a quiet room; he's often on a noisy job site or a moving train with limited time and one-handed mobile use.

Primary Need

A hierarchy that prioritizes My Schedule and Live Scores above promotional content.

To move beyond my own assumptions, I asked the 10 participants to rank common app features from 1 (Critical) to 4 (Secondary). This data directly informed the new Information Architecture, ensuring the most vital data required the fewest taps.

04 / ITERATION

Learning from Failed Iterations

I used 'Crazy 8’s' to ideate rapidly. My first designs actually failed—they created new issues. I dove back into wireframing and settled on a design that leveraged existing mobile habits. Users settled on a layout because it felt familiar and offered easy access to data.

Nav Shift

Moved from hidden side-menu to standard 4-tab bottom navigation.

IA Cleanup

Consolidated 12 menu items into 4 logical buckets based on user priority.

The Winner's Logic

Users preferred the final design for three reasons: Familiarity (reduced learning curve), Engaging Graphics, and Immediate Data Access.

Logic

Thumb-Zone: Core menu moved to bottom nav for one-handed use on job sites.

Logic

Familiarity: Standardized 'Home' and 'Search' icons to reduce learning curves.

Logic

Priority: Sticky "Live Now" header satisfies the need for immediate data.

User Reservation: While highly efficient, some users felt the "Exclusive Content" was deprioritized. However, 100% of participants agreed that score accessibility was the primary goal.

Mapping the Breakdown of Logic

The core issue wasn't just aesthetic; the app suffered from Information Architecture (IA) collapse. After the October 2021 update, users were left without a persistent navigation anchor. This forced fans into a "loop of frustration," where they had to back out to the home screen multiple times just to switch between news and scores.

The Legacy Logic (Broken)

A confusing 4-level deep hierarchy. Users had to open a side-drawer and dig through nested menus just to find basic match info.

PAIN POINT: 4+ CLICKS TO ENTRY

The New Logic (Streamlined)

A flat, 1-level hierarchy using a persistent Bottom Tab Bar. Core features are now visible and accessible instantly.

SOLUTION: 1 CLICK ACCESSIBILITY
05 / IMPACT

Impact & Conclusion

To validate the redesign, I returned to my original 10 participants for a High-Fidelity Usability Test. I measured the same core task: Find a specific match lineup. The results moved from a "Functional Lockout" to a frictionless experience, proving that a user-centered Information Architecture (IA) is the backbone of app retention.

Takeaway: Don’t be scared to start over; there is no point in forcing a concept that is wrong at its core. Instead, let your creativity flow and embrace imperfections, as the best solutions often arrive when you least expect them. Draw inspiration from established designs to build familiarity and reduce user fatigue, but never stop validating—test everything, always, because there is no such thing as too much testing.
